Bloch Sphere

The general spin \(\frac{1}{2}\) state $$ \ket{\psi} = \cos \frac{\theta}{2} \ket{\uparrow} + e^{i \phi} \sin \frac{\theta}{2} \ket{\downarrow} $$ suggests a representation on the 3-dimensional unit sphere

State \( \ket{\psi} \) is represented by the point $$\newcommand\mycolv[1]{\begin{bmatrix}#1\end{bmatrix}}$$ $$ \vec{n} = \mycolv{\sin \theta \cos \phi\\ \sin \theta \sin \phi\\ \cos \theta} $$ on the bloch sphere.
Note: Point on opposite sides correspond to orthogonal states $$ \braket{\psi_\vec{n}| \psi_{-\vec{n}}} = 0 $$ Note: The coordinates of \( \vec{n} \) correspond to the expectation values of the spin operators $$ \vec{n} = \frac{2}{\hbar} \braket{\psi | \hat{S} | \psi} = \braket{\psi | \hat{\sigma} | \psi} = \mycolv{\braket{\psi | \hat{\sigma}_x | \psi}\\\braket{\psi | \hat{\sigma}_y | \psi}\\ \braket{\psi | \hat{\sigma}_z | \psi}} $$
